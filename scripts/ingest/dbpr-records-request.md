**Subject:** Public records request — real estate licensee email addresses

To the Custodian of Public Records:

Under Article I, Section 24 of the Florida Constitution and Chapter 119, Florida
Statutes, I request a copy of the following public record:

A data export of all **active real estate sales associates and brokers**
licensed by the Division of Real Estate, containing for each licensee:

1. Licensee name
2. License number
3. License type and status
4. County
5. **Email address of record** (as furnished under §455.275(1), Florida Statutes)

I am requesting the same population covered by the "Real Estate Sales Associates
and Brokers" CSV published on the Division's public records page, with the email
address field included.

Email addresses provided to an agency are public records under Chapter 119
unless a specific statutory exemption applies. I am not requesting records for
any licensee exempt under §119.071(4)(d), Florida Statutes, and I ask that any
such records simply be withheld rather than delaying the remainder of the
request.

I would prefer the record in electronic format (CSV or Excel) delivered by email
or download link, which should minimize the cost of production.

Please advise in advance if the estimated cost of production will exceed
$[AMOUNT], and provide an itemized estimate. If any portion of this request is
denied, please cite the specific statutory exemption relied upon, as required by
§119.07(1)(e), Florida Statutes.

Thank you for your assistance.

## Notes

* Ask for **CSV/electronic delivery** — DBPR already publishes this population
  as CSV weekly, so adding one column should be cheap. Paper or per-page copy
  charges are what make these requests expensive.
* Set a real dollar figure in the cost cap so they contact you instead of
  quietly running up a bill.
* If they refuse on the grounds that emails are used for official communication,
  the relevant point is that §455.275(1) makes the address part of the licensure
  record; the narrow exemptions in §119.071 cover protected professions
  (law enforcement, judges, and similar), not real-estate licensees.
* Same playbook applies to the other states listed under "Paths to grow further"
  — PA Right-to-Know is the next biggest.
