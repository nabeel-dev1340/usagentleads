A CRM is the operating system of a real estate business. It's where your leads live, your follow-ups get scheduled, your pipeline gets tracked, and your deals move from first contact to closing table. Without one, you're running a six- or seven-figure business on sticky notes and memory.

But here's the problem: the real estate CRM market is crowded, noisy, and full of platforms that claim to be "built for agents" while offering watered-down versions of generic sales software. We spent three weeks evaluating the five most widely used platforms, testing each one against the workflows that actually matter in a working agent's day.

This is not a sponsored review. We don't earn affiliate commissions from any CRM listed here. We're a data company — we want you to have the best tools to use with the contact data we provide.

## How We Evaluated Each CRM

We scored each platform across six categories that matter most to real estate professionals:

| Criteria | What We Tested | Weight |
|----------|---------------|--------|
| Ease of Setup | Time from signup to first contact imported and first task created | 20% |
| Contact Management | Custom fields, tagging, segmentation, duplicate handling, import/export | 20% |
| Follow-Up Automation | Drip campaigns, task sequences, reminders, birthday/anniversary triggers | 20% |
| Lead Routing & Speed | How quickly new leads get assigned and contacted (critical for teams) | 15% |
| Mobile Experience | App quality, offline access, speed, calling/texting from app | 15% |
| Value for Money | Features per dollar, hidden fees, contract requirements | 10% |

<Callout type="info" title="What We Didn't Test">
We focused on CRM functionality — not website builders, IDX integration, or marketing automation suites. Some platforms (like kvCORE) bundle these features, but we evaluated them purely as contact and deal management tools. A great CRM with a mediocre website builder is still a great CRM.
</Callout>

## 1. Follow Up Boss — Best for Teams

**Starting at $58/user/month**

Follow Up Boss has become the default CRM for high-performing real estate teams, and after testing it, we understand why. Its entire design philosophy revolves around one metric: speed to lead.

When a new lead comes in — from Zillow, Realtor.com, your website, a landing page, or any of 250+ integrated sources — Follow Up Boss routes it to the right agent in under 10 seconds and triggers an automated action plan (call + text + email sequence). For teams where the first agent to respond wins the deal, this is transformative.

### What stands out

- **Smart Lists** — dynamically filter your database by last contact date, lead source, pipeline stage, or any custom field. This is your daily "who should I call?" list, and it updates automatically.
- **Calling and texting built in** — calls are logged, recorded (optional), and transcribed. Texts send from your business number. No switching between apps.
- **Reporting by agent** — see exactly how many leads each team member contacted, how fast they responded, and how many they converted. Accountability without micromanagement.
- **Pond system** — unclaimed or unresponsive leads go into a shared "pond" that other agents can grab. No leads rot in someone's pipeline.

### Where it falls short

- **No transaction management** — you'll need a separate tool (Dotloop, SkySlope, or similar) for document tracking and closing checklists
- **Per-user pricing adds up** — a 10-person team is paying $580+/month before any add-ons
- **Overkill for solo agents** — the team-centric features don't add value if you're working alone

**Our take:** If you run a team of 3+ agents and lead response time is critical to your business, Follow Up Boss is the clear winner. The ROI math is simple: if the speed-to-lead advantage closes even one extra deal per quarter, the CRM pays for itself 10x over.

## 2. LionDesk — Best for Solo Agents

**Starting at $25/month**

LionDesk is the CRM equivalent of a Swiss Army knife — it does a lot of things reasonably well at a price that won't stress a solo agent's budget. Its standout feature is video email: you can record a personal video message and embed it directly in your outreach emails and texts.

### What stands out

- **Video email and video texting** — record directly in the app, embed in emails with a thumbnail that plays on click. Open rates on video emails run 2-3x higher than plain text.
- **AI-powered follow-up assistant** — "Gabby" (their AI) can respond to inbound leads with natural-sounding texts and emails, buying you time when you're at a showing.
- **Power dialer** — built-in calling with automatic logging. Click through your call list without touching your phone.
- **Affordable** — $25/month gets you unlimited contacts, drip campaigns, and the video features. No per-user scaling.

### Where it falls short

- **Interface feels dated** — the UI hasn't had a major refresh in a while. Functional but not inspiring.
- **Limited integrations** — doesn't connect to as many lead sources as Follow Up Boss. You may need Zapier for some workflows.
- **Reporting is basic** — you get the essentials but nothing approaching the analytics depth of team-focused platforms.

<Callout type="tip" title="The Video Email Edge">
In our testing, video emails sent through LionDesk averaged 42% open rates compared to 28% for identical plain-text versions. If you're a solo agent whose personality is a selling point (and in real estate, it almost always is), the video feature alone justifies the subscription.
</Callout>

**Our take:** Best value CRM on the market for individual agents. If you're working solo and want one tool that handles contacts, follow-ups, calling, and texting without breaking the bank, LionDesk delivers.

## 3. kvCORE (Inside Real Estate) — Best for Brokerages

**Custom pricing (typically $300-500+/month for teams)**

kvCORE isn't really a CRM — it's an all-in-one real estate operating platform that happens to include a CRM. It bundles an IDX website, lead generation tools, marketing automation, listing marketing, and CRM into a single system. It's the platform powering many large brokerages and franchise operations.

### What stands out

- **Behavioral automation** — the system tracks what leads are doing on your website (which listings they view, how often they return, what price range they search) and triggers automated responses based on behavior. A lead who views the same listing three times gets a different follow-up than a first-time visitor.
- **Squeeze pages and landing pages** — built-in lead capture pages for Facebook ads, Google ads, or organic traffic. No separate landing page tool needed.
- **Market reports for farming** — automated monthly market reports branded to you, sent to your sphere. Great for geographic farming.
- **Enterprise admin controls** — brokerage admins can manage agent accounts, redistribute leads when agents leave, and monitor adoption across the office.

### Where it falls short

- **Steep learning curve** — kvCORE is powerful but complex. Expect 2-4 weeks before you're comfortable, and some features require admin setup.
- **Cost** — this is a premium platform. Individual agents rarely pay out of pocket — it's typically provided (and subsidized) by the brokerage.
- **Walled garden** — because it does everything, it doesn't integrate as well with external tools. Moving away from kvCORE means migrating everything at once.

**Our take:** If your brokerage provides kvCORE, use it — it's a powerful system once you climb the learning curve. If you're paying out of pocket as a solo agent, the cost-to-value ratio doesn't compete with purpose-built CRMs like Follow Up Boss or LionDesk.

## 4. Wise Agent — Best Bang for the Buck

**$49/month flat rate (unlimited users)**

Wise Agent is the CRM that real estate insiders love but marketing rarely reaches. It's been around since 2002 (ancient by proptech standards), and it's built by agents for agents. The flat-rate pricing — $49/month regardless of team size — is its most disruptive feature.

### What stands out

- **Transaction management included** — unlike Follow Up Boss, Wise Agent includes full transaction tracking with checklists, document storage, and deadline management. No need for a separate tool.
- **Flat pricing** — $49/month for unlimited users. A 5-person team pays the same as a solo agent. This alone makes it the most cost-effective option for small teams.
- **24/7 live support** — real humans, available around the clock. At this price point, that's unheard of.
- **Contact enrichment** — automatically pulls in social media profiles and public information for your contacts.

### Where it falls short

- **UI needs modernization** — the interface works but looks like it was designed in 2015. Younger agents may find it visually uninspiring.
- **Limited lead source integrations** — not as many native integrations as Follow Up Boss. You'll need Zapier for some lead sources.
- **No built-in calling** — you'll need a separate dialer or use your phone.

<Callout type="tip" title="The Hidden Gem for Small Teams">
If you're a team of 2-5 agents, Wise Agent is dramatically cheaper than any competitor. Follow Up Boss would cost $290-580/month for the same team. kvCORE would be $300+. Wise Agent is $49 — flat. And you get transaction management included, which most competitors charge separately for.
</Callout>

**Our take:** Wise Agent is the most underrated CRM in real estate. If you can look past the dated interface, you get more functionality per dollar than any other platform. Particularly strong for small teams and agents who want transaction management built in.

## 5. HubSpot CRM (Free Tier) — Best for Tech-Savvy Agents

**Free (paid tiers start at $20/month)**

HubSpot isn't built for real estate — it's a general-purpose CRM used by millions of businesses across every industry. But its free tier is genuinely powerful, and agents who are comfortable customizing pipelines and fields can build a highly effective system at zero cost.

### What stands out

- **Free for up to 1,000,000 contacts** — not a trial, not a limited demo. The free tier is permanent and includes core CRM features.
- **Email open and click tracking** — know exactly when a contact opens your email and which links they click. Free.
- **Meeting scheduler** — Calendly-like scheduling built into HubSpot. Share a link, let contacts book time with you. Free.
- **App marketplace** — 1,500+ integrations. If it exists, it probably connects to HubSpot.
- **Scales gracefully** — when you outgrow the free tier, paid tiers add marketing automation, sequences, and advanced reporting. You don't need to migrate to a new platform.

### Where it falls short

- **No real estate-specific features** — no MLS integration, no transaction management, no listing marketing. You build everything from scratch using custom properties and pipelines.
- **Setup time** — plan to spend 3-5 hours setting up your deal stages, contact properties, and automations to match real estate workflows.
- **Advanced features are expensive** — HubSpot's paid tiers ($20-$800+/month) scale quickly. The free tier is incredible; the jump to paid is steep.

**Our take:** If you're tech-comfortable, enjoy customizing systems, and want to start at literally $0, HubSpot is a legitimate option. You'll need to invest upfront time to configure it for real estate, but the result is a CRM that works exactly the way you want it to.

## How to Choose: The Decision Tree

Rather than reading 5 reviews and feeling more confused, answer these three questions:

**1. Are you solo or on a team?**
- Solo → LionDesk or HubSpot Free
- Team of 2-5 → Wise Agent (unbeatable value)
- Team of 5+ → Follow Up Boss
- Brokerage-provided → Use what they give you (likely kvCORE)

**2. What's your budget tolerance?**
- $0 → HubSpot Free
- Under $50/month → LionDesk or Wise Agent
- $50-100/user/month → Follow Up Boss
- Brokerage covers it → kvCORE

**3. What's your technical comfort level?**
- "Just make it work" → LionDesk or Wise Agent
- "I like tinkering" → HubSpot
- "I need admin control for my team" → Follow Up Boss or kvCORE

<Callout type="warning" title="The Biggest CRM Mistake">
The most expensive CRM mistake isn't choosing the wrong platform — it's choosing the right platform and not using it. A $25/month CRM used daily beats a $500/month CRM used sporadically. Start with the simplest tool that meets your minimum requirements. You can always upgrade later; you can't get back the months of lost follow-ups.
</Callout>

## Getting the Most From Any CRM

Regardless of which platform you choose, the value of a CRM is directly proportional to the quality of data inside it. Import your sphere of influence, past clients, and leads on day one. Then expand systematically with verified contact data — whether that's agent databases for B2B outreach, or consumer leads for buyer/seller campaigns.

A CRM full of verified, segmented contacts — with automated follow-up sequences running behind the scenes — is a revenue engine that works while you're at showings, writing offers, and closing deals.
